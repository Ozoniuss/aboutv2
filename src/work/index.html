Working together means it's important so share some core values. Very briefly, mine would be:

  • curiosity
  • data
  • trust
  • efficiency
  • meaning
  • passion
  • solve
Which is basically "cd temps" if you just take the first letter. Easy to remember. Let's briefly discuss each.


Unfortunately, we work in a field where we can't afford to stagnate. Technology is always changing at a very fast pace and in order to adapt, we need to stay curious. There is no point where you know enough to stop learning.

I'm a data-driven person. I understand that some decisions have to be made based on feeling, but when you have data available, you should use it to your advantage.

I find the most important trait of a high-performing team is not good processes but rather trust amongst its members. That means owning both the good things and the bad things. We should cherish achievements together but we should also accept that we sometimes make mistakes, and share the learnings from failures.

I value my time and I'm sure you value yours. I'm obsessed with trying to use my time in the most efficient way possible. If you end up stuck for 30 minutes once, there will always be something else to work on. If it keeps happening, we need to discuss what needs to change in the process.

I care about working on software that is meaningful. The journey of building will always be fun, but nothing replaces the feeling you get when you see your work actually being useful to other people.

I like working with people that are passionate about what they do. There's a certain kind of energy you get from talking to them. It just makes you feel more energized.

I have always been a problem solver. Not sure if that comes from years of maths contests, but I get very excited from finding a solution to a difficult problem. To me, that's what software engineering is all about in the end, and I think we should remind ourselves what the end goal is before we get attached to our own solutions.
